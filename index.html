About Me - academic side

I am currently a MSE PhD candidate at UC Berkeley, working in Prof. Jeffrey Neaton's group. My research focus is to understand the effect of imperfections (such as cation disorder and defects), interfaces, phonons and dimensionality to the spectroscopic properties in novel solid state materials using first principles calculations. I enjoy working closely with other theorists and experimentalists.
